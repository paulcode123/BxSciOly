## Section 1: Fundamental Concepts (10 points)

**1. C (4)**
*Carbon typically forms 4 covalent bonds in stable organic structures due to its four valence electrons (sp3 hybridization in diamond/alkanes, sp2 in graphene/alkenes).*

**2. Dimensionality is distinguished by how many dimensions are outside the nanoscale (>100 nm).**
- **0D:** All three dimensions are at the nanoscale (<100 nm).
- **1D:** Two dimensions are at the nanoscale, one is macroscopic.
- **2D:** One dimension is at the nanoscale, two are macroscopic.
- **3D:** No dimensions are at the nanoscale (bulk material with nano-features).

**3. The energy difference between the top of the valence band and the bottom of the conduction band.**
*In this region, no electronic states can exist within the material.*

**4. A (Localized Surface Plasmon Resonance)**
*LSPR is the collective oscillation of electrons in metal nanoparticles when excited by light, causing strong absorption and scattering at specific wavelengths.*

**5. A (sp2)**
*Graphene and carbon nanotubes consist of carbon atoms arranged in a hexagonal lattice, where each carbon is bonded to three others via sp2 hybridization.*

**6. Doping is the intentional introduction of impurities into an intrinsic (pure) semiconductor.**
*This is done to modulate its electrical properties, creating either n-type (excess electrons) or p-type (excess holes) semiconductors.*

## Section 2: Nanomaterials and Applications (8 points)

**7. D (It increases the surface area-to-volume ratio, and increases the surface reactivities)**
*Smaller size leads to a much higher percentage of atoms being located at the surface, which increases catalytic activity and reactivity.*

**8. Andre Geim and Konstantin Novoselov.**
*They were awarded the prize for "ground-breaking experiments regarding the two-dimensional material graphene."*

**9. Based on the approach to fabrication:**
- **Top-down:** Starting from bulk material and removing material to create nanostructures (e.g., lithography, milling, etching).
- **Bottom-up:** Building materials atom-by-atom or molecule-by-molecule (e.g., chemical vapor deposition, self-assembly).

**10. C (Larger size associates with longer wavelengths absorbed)**
*Due to quantum confinement, larger quantum dots have smaller bandgaps (red-shifted emission/absorption), while smaller dots have larger bandgaps (blue-shifted).*

## Section 3: Material Properties and Structure (10 points)

**11. Hall-Petch Equation Variables:**
- **σy:** Yield stress (Yield strength) [Pa or N/m²]
- **σ₀:** Starting stress for dislocation movement (friction stress) [Pa or N/m²]
- **ky:** Locking parameter (strengthening coefficient) [Pa·m^1/2 or N/m^3/2]
- **d:** Average grain diameter [m]

**12. Diamond Structure Analysis:**
- **Poor Conductivity:** Diamond has sp3 hybridization, forming four strong localized sigma bonds in a 3D tetrahedral network. This leaves no free delocalized electrons (unlike sp2 graphene) to carry current, making it an insulator.
- **Durability:** The extreme hardness and durability come from the rigid, 3D covalent bond network which requires immense energy to deform or break.

**13. Intermolecular Forces:**
- **Adhesion:** Attraction between molecules of different substances (e.g., water sticking to a glass slide).
- **Cohesion:** Attraction between molecules of the same substance (e.g., water molecules sticking to each other).

**14. A (Isotropic)**
*Amorphous materials lack long-range order, so their physical properties are the same regardless of the direction in which they are measured.*

**17. Interplanar Spacing Calculation:**
- Given: $\lambda = 0.200$ nm, $\theta = 45^\circ$, $n = 1$ (assumed first-order).
- Bragg's Law: $n\lambda = 2d \sin(\theta)$
- $0.200 = 2d \sin(45^\circ)$
- $0.200 = 2d (0.707)$
- $0.200 = 1.414d$
- **$d = 0.141$ nm**

## Section 5: Calculations (16 points)

**18. SA:V Ratio Theory:**
- **a) Definition:** The ratio of the surface area of an object to its volume. It measures how much surface area is available per unit of bulk material.
- **b) Sphere Derivation:**
  - $SA = 4\pi r^2$
  - $V = \frac{4}{3}\pi r^3$
  - $SA:V = \frac{4\pi r^2}{\frac{4}{3}\pi r^3} = \frac{3}{r}$

**19. Carbon Fiber Calculation:**
- Given: $r = 30$ nm, $L = 100$ nm.
- $SA = 2\pi r^2 + 2\pi rL = 2\pi(30)^2 + 2\pi(30)(100) = 1800\pi + 6000\pi = 7800\pi$ nm²
- $V = \pi r^2 L = \pi(30)^2(100) = 90000\pi$ nm³
- $SA:V = \frac{7800\pi}{90000\pi} = \frac{78}{900}$
- **$SA:V = 0.087$ nm⁻¹**

**20. Elastic Modulus (CNT Annulus):**
- $Area = \pi(R_{out}^2 - R_{in}^2) = \pi(3^2 - 1.5^2) = 6.75\pi$ nm² $\approx 21.21$ nm²
- Force = $3$ nN
- Stress ($\sigma$) = $F/A = 3 / 21.21 \text{ nN/nm}^2 = 0.1415$ GPa
- Strain ($\epsilon$) = $0.01\% = 0.0001$
- $E = \sigma / \epsilon = 0.1415$ GPa / $0.0001$
- **$E = 1415$ GPa (or 1.42 TPa)**

**21. A (1 × 10¹² Pa)**
- $Area = \pi(0.03)^2 = 0.0009\pi \approx 0.002827$ m²
- Stress = $500 / 0.002827 \approx 176,838$ Pa
- Strain = $177 \text{ nm} / 1 \text{ m} = 1.77 \times 10^{-7}$
- $E = 176,838 / 1.77 \times 10^{-7} \approx 9.99 \times 10^{11}$ Pa $\approx 10^{12}$ Pa.

**22. C (0.462 nm⁻¹)**
- $r = d/2 = 6.5$ nm
- $SA:V = 3/r = 3/6.5 = 0.4615$ nm⁻¹.
