I'm running for co-captain of the Science Olympiad team at Bronx Science.
I want to make a demo on this website that will present my platform in a really cool way, and also integrate it with the website.

Here is my platform:

1. I will make the team more competitive: this year we got 19th in States, down from 12th last year.
2. Currently, during meetings, Event Managers give detached slideshow presentations on the events and its well known that many people don't pay attention, and don't learn anything.
3. My plan is to have people do the learning part at home, on this website, and then during the meetings we can have a more interactive experience based on mutual understanding. When everyone in the event has invested time and effort into learning the material, when they come together, it sparks excitement and energy.
4. Many of you, the seniors, have said that over your time in SciOly, you don't feel like you've learned very much- that it's mostly just a memory game.
5. This may be true of the tests, to some extent, but it doesn't have to be true of our events. With this website, we can efficiently and effectively teach our members the conceptual groundwork of the events, before layering on the "trivia" part.
6. With this website, I've also automated much of the logistics of running the team.
    a. Members can seamlessly manage events, take attendance, apply to competitions, share tests, binders, and so much more.
7. The fact is, we have a lot of talent on this team, and we need to be able to tap into it. And without some of the radical changes I'm proposing, we won't be able to do that. Next year, I have one goal, and one shot: to have our team get top 10 in States.
8. You may be wondering, who am I to make these changes? Well 2 years ago, I founded the SciWeb Initiative, which has evolved into a platform where people can learn just about anything incredibly quickly through concept map architecture paired with State-space AI.
    a. In short, what we figured out is that the human brain is a state machine, storing information not in sentences, but in a network of interconnected concepts.
    b. When we use AI tools to transcribe the user's speech and ideas and integrate them into this map, we can then directly challenge and probe the user's knowledge.
    c. I plan to deploy this technology to Science Olympiad, boosting our team's ability to learn and retain advanced material.
9. If its not clear by now, I want SciOly to be a memorable and life-changing experience for everyone involved. This is how we can make that happen.
