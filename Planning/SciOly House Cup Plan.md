## Background

- One of our top priorities this year is to make SciOly more fun  
- We also want our members to have as much realistic competition experience as possible to prepare them for regionals & states and to evaluate their knowledge and test-taking skills well in advance  
- Many people have also said that our members should have experience working with the same partner consistently so that they can practice splitting up the test, getting to know each other’s strengths, helping each other answer questions, etc.  
- But beyond just working in pairs, and regardless of how many competitions we go to, we also want to give people the feeling of working for a team, a greater cause of sorts  
- *Basically, we need to give everyone the real Science Olympiad competitive experience, regardless of whether they actually make it to competition*

## Proposal

Introducing, The House Cup

1. Near the beginning of the year, we split everyone into one of 6 houses within SciOly  
   1. Each one has a different name, maybe named after a Scientist, etc.  
   2. All of them have some sort of team bonding going on(team bracelets, chant, motto, etc.) and a team captain responsible for motivating the members to excel in their studying(maybe an EM)  
   3. Maybe we have a sorting ceremony, Hogwarts style…  
   4. Depending on how many people we accept into SciOly next year, that means about 25-30 members in each house  
2. In each event, there is 1 pair per house  
   1. Totals to 12 people per event(we can modify this by having multiple pairs for some events or changing the number of houses)  
   2. The responsibility of SDs and EMs remains the same: they mentor everyone in the event, making sure that everyone stays on the same page and is able to learn to their fullest potential  
3. Houses compete for points: the house with the most points at the end of the year is awarded the house cup at the end of year celebration. Points are awarded through:  
   1. Full-meeting tests(every 2-3 weeks): a test is made or questions taken from online and the score each pair gets is added to their house total  
   2. Meeting activities & SD discretion: kahoots, deep research presentations, etc.  
   3. Individual Studying: Learning modules completed on the bxscioly.org website offer small amounts of points as well  
   4. Real medals in competitions  
   5. Individuals with the most points at the end of the year are also individually recognized  
4. By the time Regionals comes around and this has been going on for a couple of months, the members should be used to competing with their partners, working for a team, and we can select the best pairs across all of the houses with ample performance data on each to represent the entire SciOly team in Regionals and States.
