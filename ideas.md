1. attendance: people enter a day and event specific code on the site to check in(ofc people can send it to their friends but just note that attendance may also be checked by leadership)
2. After people upload a bunch of tests for a given event and the questions have all been parsed, there will be a feature that iterates through all the questions, groups them, and maps the groups in relation to each other.
3. We want our members to learn as much as possible: that's what SciOly is all about. That means not just building a massive binder, but actually learning the theory of it all
4. When someone signs up for an event, they will start by doing an AI-administered knowledge survey. The survey will create a learning plan that is specific to them and their event and establishes a solid foundation that they can use to master the event.
5. Over this plan they will build out a personalized concept map for the event.
6. They can then map any question, idea, or excerpt onto their concept map to see how it fits in.
7. After completing the learning plan, they will start compiling a binder that extends from their concept map and includes all the information they need to answer all of the questions in the event.
