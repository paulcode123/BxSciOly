Classification & Compilation Events

All events are unlimited binder events

Rocks and Minerals

Description: Teams identify rocks and minerals and demonstrate their knowledge of geological processes, formation, and classification systems.

Type: Identification Event
Resources: Unlimited binder
Team Size: 2 members

Key Skills:

  • Mineral identification using physical properties
  • Rock classification (igneous, sedimentary, metamorphic)
  • Geological formation processes and environments
  • Crystal systems and mineral structures
  • Field identification techniques
  • Economic and industrial uses of rocks and minerals
Event Manager: TBD

Entomology

Description: Competitors will be assessed on their knowledge of North American insect families, focusing mostly on identification but also including ecology and behavior.

Type: Identification Event
Focus: North American insects
Team Size: 2 members

Key Skills:

  • Insect taxonomy and family classification
  • Morphological identification features
  • Life cycles and metamorphosis types
  • Ecological roles and habitat preferences
  • Insect behavior and social structures
  • Economic importance and pest management
Event Manager: TBD

Astronomy

Description: Teams will demonstrate understanding of Stellar Evolution: Star Formation & Exoplanets, including classification, life cycles, and planetary systems.

Type: Study Event
Focus: Stellar evolution & exoplanets
Team Size: 2 members

Key Skills:

  • Stellar classification and Hertzsprung-Russell diagram
  • Star formation processes and stellar nurseries
  • Stellar evolution and life cycles
  • Exoplanet detection methods and characteristics
  • Planetary system formation and dynamics
  • Astronomical observation and data interpretation
Event Manager: TBD

Team Leadership

Subject Director

1 Subject Director leads the Research subject, responsible for developing research methodologies and identification skills.

Responsibilities:

  • Teaching research and compilation techniques
  • Developing identification and classification skills
  • Leading binder construction workshops
  • Coordinating specimen study sessions

Event Managers

Each event has exactly 1 Event Manager responsible for organizational and communicative tasks.

Responsibilities:

  • Getting to know members on a personal level
  • Creating meeting activities and competitions
  • Facilitating learning and practice
  • Organizing study materials and specimens
