Biology Events

Anatomy and Physiology

Description: Participants will be assessed on their understanding of the anatomy and physiology for the human Nervous, Sensory, and Endocrine systems.

Type: Study Event
Focus: Nervous, Sensory, Endocrine
Team Size: 2 members

Key Skills:

  • Nervous system structure and function
  • Sensory organ anatomy and physiology
  • Endocrine system and hormone regulation
  • Neurological pathways and reflexes
  • Homeostasis and feedback mechanisms
  • Clinical applications and disorders

Disease Detectives

Description: Participants will use their investigative skills in the scientific study of disease, injury, health, and disability in populations or groups of people.

Type: Investigation Event
Focus: Epidemiological analysis
Team Size: 2 members

Key Skills:

  • Epidemiological investigation methods
  • Disease outbreak analysis and tracking
  • Statistical analysis of health data
  • Population health assessment
  • Risk factor identification and analysis
  • Public health intervention strategies
Event Manager: TBD

Designer Genes

Description: Participants will solve problems and analyze data or diagrams using their knowledge of the basic principles of genetics, molecular genetics and biotechnology.

Type: Problem-solving Event
Focus: Genetics & biotechnology
Team Size: 2 members

Key Skills:

  • Mendelian genetics and inheritance patterns
  • Molecular genetics and DNA structure
  • Biotechnology techniques and applications
  • Genetic engineering and CRISPR technology
  • Protein synthesis and gene expression
  • Genetic disorders and therapeutic approaches

Team Leadership

Subject Directors

2 Subject Directors lead the Biology subject, responsible for mastering biological concepts and teaching methodologies.

Responsibilities:

  • Teaching human biology and genetics concepts
  • Developing expertise in anatomy and epidemiology
  • Leading laboratory and practical sessions
  • Coordinating study materials and resources

Event Managers

Each event has exactly 1 Event Manager responsible for organizational and communicative tasks.

Responsibilities:

  • Getting to know members on a personal level
  • Creating meeting activities and competitions
  • Facilitating learning and practice
  • Organizing laboratory materials and specimens
