QUESTION: A polar orbiting satellite is located at an altitude of 300 miles (483 km) above the surface of the Earth. What is its orbital velocity?

---QUESTION 12---

TOPIC: Remote Sensing | Spatial Resolution | Instantaneous Field of View
DIFFICULTY: Very High
QUESTION: A satellite with a polar orbit 100 miles (160 km) above the surface of the Earth has a MWIR (3 – 5 µm) imaging system with a one megapixel (1024 × 1024) format, a pixel pitch of 50 µm, and a 100 mm diameter lens with a focal length of 1250 mm. ---QUESTION 54---
ID: 54 | POINTS: 4.00 | FORMAT: Calculation | Stefan-Boltzmann Law
TOPIC: Climate Science | Stellar Physics | Solar Luminosity
DIFFICULTY: High
QUESTION: NO CREDIT IF WORK IS NOT SHOWN. The photosphere (visible surface of the Sun) has a temperature of roughly 5770 K. The radius of the Sun is 696,000 km. What is the luminosity of the Sun in watts?
CALCULATION: L = 4πR²σT⁴ where σ = 5.67 × 10⁻⁸ W/m²K⁴, R = 6.96 × 10⁸ m, T = 5770 K

---QUESTION 55---
ID: 55 | POINTS: 4.00 | FORMAT: Calculation | Solar Constant
TOPIC: Climate Science | Planetary Energy Balance | Solar Flux
DIFFICULTY: High
QUESTION: Mars has a radius of 3390 km, and is 228,000,000 km from the Sun. What is the "solar constant" for Mars (radiant flux density in W/m²)? Use the accepted Sun luminosity of 3.828 × 10²⁶ W, not the answer you got in the previous problem.
CALCULATION: Solar constant = L / (4πd²) where d = 2.28 × 10¹¹ m

---QUESTION 56---
