Kinetics Free Response (40 points)
An Introduction to Enzyme Kinetics
Traditionally, chemistry is taught with a strong emphasis on thermodynamics and equilibrium. While
thermodynamics predicts what will happen in the long run, kinetics explains what actually happens in the
moment. For slow reactions, kinetics are most important, and the discussion of equilibrium becomes largely
irrelevant. As the economist John Maynard Keynes famously said, “In the long run, we are all dead.”
Catalysts play a particularly crucial role in biological systems. In biology, these catalysts are known
as enzymes. The study of enzyme kinetics, particularly Michaelis–Menten kinetics, is fundamental to un-
derstanding biochemistry. Typically, the conversion of a substrate (reactant) into a product by an enzyme
follows the mechanism with two elementary steps described below:
