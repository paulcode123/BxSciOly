Write out your answer to the following questions
1. (5 points) Explain why a compound machine may have a very high IMA but still be inefficient
in practice.
2. (5 points) Describe how potential energy and kinetic energy trade off in a non-equilibrium
pulley system when the load accelerates.
3. (5 points) Explain why in compound machines the total mechanical advantage multiplies, but
the efficiency always decreases compared to each individual stage.
4. (6 points) Explain why a machine can increase force but cannot increase the total amount
of work done. In your answer, include the relationship between input work, output work,
and efficiency, and describe how this applies to the definition of Ideal Mechanical Advantage
(IMA).
5. (7 points) Define both IMA and AMA. Derive the equation for efficiency in terms of these two
quantities, and explain why AMA is always less than or equal to IMA.
6. (6 points) Derive the formula for the IMA of an inclined plane. State your assumptions clearly
(ignore friction). Then explain how the efficiency would change if friction were included and
how this affects the AMA.
7. (6 points) Explain how the Ideal Mechanical Advantage of a pulley system can be determined
by counting rope segments. Why does this method work? Justify mathematically using the
conservation of work principle.
8. (5 points) Why does friction always reduce the efficiency of a machine? Hint: relate friction
to input work, output work, and wasted energy.
9. (6 points) Compare the effects of static and kinetic friction in simple machines. Which one
determines whether the machine starts moving, and which one governs motion after it starts?
10. (6 points) Explain why increasing the force output of a machine always requires a propor-
tional increase in the input distance. Derive this from the definition of work and show the
force–distance tradeoff explicitly.
