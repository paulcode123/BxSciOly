---QUESTION 12---
ID: 12
POINTS: 2.00
FORMAT: Free Response | Equilibrium explanation
TOPIC: Equilibrium | Complex ion formation | Solubility | Ammonia complexation
DIFFICULTY: High
QUESTION: Using equilibrium, explain why the solubility of Ag+ decreases as NH3 is added, then increases as excess NH3 is increased. 1-2 sentences.
ANSWER: Initially, NH3 forms Ag(NH3)⁺ complex, shifting equilibrium to dissolve more Ag⁺. With excess NH3, [Ag(NH3)⁺] increases, shifting back toward precipitation equilibrium, but excess ligand facilitates formation of Ag(NH3)₂⁺ soluble complex.
