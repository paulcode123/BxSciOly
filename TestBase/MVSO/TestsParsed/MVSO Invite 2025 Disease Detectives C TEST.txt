---QUESTIONS 23-92 (MATCHING)---
ID: 23-92 | POINTS: 1.00 each | FORMAT: Definition matching to epidemiological terms
TOPIC: Epidemiology | Definitions | Terminology | Concepts
DIFFICULTY: High
QUESTION TYPE: Match 70 epidemiological terms to their definitions

KEY TERMS TO MATCH:
- Applied Epidemiology: Putting epidemiological research methods to use in public health practice
- Confounding: Manipulation of association between exposure and health outcome by third variable
- Active Immunity: Immunity from antibodies produced in response to antigen presence
- Neonatal Mortality Rate: Deaths from birth to 28 days per 1,000 live births
- Acute Exposure: Contact with substance once or briefly
- Analytic Study: Study evaluating association between exposure to hazards and disease
- Measure of Association: Quantifies strength of statistical association between exposure and outcome
- Sentinel Surveillance: Reporting units identifying and notifying certain diseases
- Confidentiality: Duty to keep patient information secure
- Antigen: Toxin or foreign substance inducing immune response
- Attack Rate: Probability infection occurs among susceptible after contact with infectious source
- Information Bias: Bias arising from measurement error
- Selection Bias: Choice of individuals in analysis without proper randomization
- Passive Surveillance: Gathers disease data from health care workers without prompting
- Crude Birth Rate: Live births per 1,000 population per year
- Standard Deviation: Measure of variation in set of data values
- Index Case: Initial patient in epidemiological investigation
- Necessary Cause: Factor that must be present for disease to occur
- Agent: Microorganism or chemical substance essential for disease occurrence
- Class Interval: Size of each class in range of variable
- Virulency: Degree of damage caused by microbe to host
- Comparison Value: Calculated concentration unlikely to cause harmful effects
- Point-Source Outbreak: Outbreak from exposure to same source over brief time
- Case-Control Study: Study comparing exposures of people with and without disease
- Delayed Health Effect: Disease/injury from past exposures
- Detection Limit: Lowest concentration reliably distinguished from zero
- Dose-Response Relationship: Relationship between exposure amount and health changes
- Efficacy: Ability to produce intended results under ideal conditions
- Efficiency: Ability to produce intended results with minimum time/resources
- Endemic Level: Amount of disease usually present in community
- Epidemiologic Trial: Analytical study with hypothesis testing
- Descriptive Epidemiology: Study of disease amount and distribution by person, place, time
- Exposure-Dose Reconstruction: Method of estimating past exposure to hazardous substances
- Fomite: Nonliving object capable of carrying infectious organisms
- Observational Study: Researcher observes without changing people
- Health Promotion: Process enabling people to improve their health
- Healthy Worker Effect: Workers exhibit lower death rates than general population
- Synergistic Effect: Biologic response where one substance worsens another's effect
- Host Factor: Traits of individual affecting susceptibility to disease
- Representative Sample: Sample whose characteristics match original population
- Herd Immunity: Immunity in population based on acquired immunity in high proportion
- Experimental Study: Researcher intervenes to change something and observes results
- Passive Immunity: Short-term immunity from antibodies from another person/animal
- Incidence: Number of new disease cases in population over specific time
- Incubation Period: Period between exposure and onset of clinical symptoms
- Risk Communication: Exchange of information to increase understanding of health risks
- In Vitro: Artificial environment outside living organism
- Vectorborne Transmission: Infections transmitted by infected arthropod bite
- In Vivo: Within living organism
- Crude Mortality Rate: Mortality from all causes per population per time period
- Infant Mortality Rate: Deaths of infants under one year per 1,000 live births
- Teratogen: Substance causing defects in development before birth
- Random Sample: Items drawn so each item has equal opportunity to appear
- Postneonatal Mortality Rate: Deaths from 28 days to one year per 1,000 births
- Normal Distribution: Symmetrical bell-shaped graph function
- Vehicleborne Transmission: Infections from inanimate object/material
- Odds Ratio: Measure of association between exposure and outcome
- Outbreak: Sudden increase in disease occurrences in particular time/place
- Common-Source Outbreak: Group exposed to same infectious agent or toxin source
- Pandemic: Epidemic spread through human populations across large region
- Airborne Transmission: Bacteria/viruses on dust/respiratory droplets
- Prevalence: Number of existing disease cases in population at specific time
- Reservoir: Habitat where infectious agent normally lives and multiplies
- Contact Tracing: Identification, monitoring, support of exposed person
- Healthy Lifestyle: Living that lowers disease risk and improves well-being
- Analytic Epidemiology: Study concerned with search for causes and effects
